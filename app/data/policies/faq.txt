FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Why was my loan application rejected?
A: Applications are rejected when two or more underwriting criteria are not met. Common reasons include low credit score, high debt-to-income ratio, insufficient income, or requesting an amount too large relative to monthly income.

Q: What does "manual review" mean?
A: Manual review means the application failed exactly one underwriting criterion. It is not an automatic rejection — a human underwriter will evaluate the full application.

Q: Can I reapply after a rejection?
A: Yes. Applicants can submit a new loan application at any time. Improving credit score or reducing debt-to-income ratio before reapplying increases the likelihood of approval.

Q: How is my interest rate determined?
A: All approved loans start at a base rate of 8.0%. Excellent credit (780+) reduces the rate by 1.0%. A debt-to-income ratio above 25% adds a 1.5% surcharge.

Q: What documents are required to apply?
A: Applicants need proof of income, identification, and consent for a credit check.
